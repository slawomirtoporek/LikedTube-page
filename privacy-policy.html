6. Retention

App state is partly stored locally using a persistence mechanism (including the Zustand library and AsyncStorage). Data includes YouTube-related cache and preferences, stored under AsyncStorage keys following the pattern video-storage-{user identifier}-{opaque suffix} (the opaque suffix is derived from your account email; the "video-storage record").

  • Firebase Authentication account data: User data associated with the account is retained until you delete the account or submit a deletion request. Your session in the App ends when you log out of the app; logging out does not automatically delete your user record on Firebase. Account data is retained until you delete your account in the App or request deletion by email. Deletion of account data held by the Controller (Firebase / Firestore) occurs when you use Account → Delete account in the App or following an email request handled by the Controller.
  • Local copy of YouTube data and related App state (video-storage record): may remain on the device after you log out of YouTube or the App. Log-out removes OAuth tokens and clears UI-visible data in RAM, but does not guarantee deletion of the video-storage file from device storage. To permanently remove the local copy: uninstall the App or on Android: Settings → Apps → {{app_name}} → Storage → Clear storage / Clear data (wording may vary by OS version).
  • Subscription data: for the duration of the subscription and any period required by law.
  • Advertising data: per Google AdMob retention policies.
  • Google Analytics for Firebase (GA4) event data: retained according to Google’s settings for the linked Analytics property. GA4 offers configurable event-data retention (e.g. 2 months or 14 months, depending on the Controller’s configuration) (set in GA4 Admin → Data Settings → Data Retention). The Developer does not maintain a separate copy of raw analytics logs outside Google’s services.
  • Other data held locally in the same record as above: same rules as for the video-storage record unless you clear app data or uninstall.

Deletion of data in Firebase takes place promptly after you complete the action, subject to technical delays in Google’s backup systems.

  • Firebase / Firestore data: delete your account in the App (Account → Delete account) or email the Controller at {{email}}.
  • Advertising ID: reset or disable it in your Android device settings (e.g. Settings → Google → Ads).
  • Google Analytics for Firebase (GA4): data is held in Google’s analytics systems. Analytics data is not used by the Controller to directly identify you. Data are processed in pseudonymised form. Google Analytics for Firebase applies measures that limit identifiability, including IP address anonymisation in line with Google’s practices. You can manage certain Google account–related data and activity controls via myaccount.google.com and Google’s privacy tools; deletion of your Firebase account (see above) does not automatically erase all historical analytics aggregates retained by Google under its policies.
